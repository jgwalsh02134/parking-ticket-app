# VERIFIED — Beating City of Albany Red-Light (RLC) & School-Zone Speed (SZS) Camera Tickets

Every statute, deadline, threshold, and defense below is from a primary source
(NYS Open Legislation, the Albany enabling bill, and the City of Albany's own
pages). The app may cite ONLY what is in this document. Never invent a statute,
case, or fact. Camera tickets are a DIFFERENT legal animal from parking tickets —
the VTL § 238 "ticket defect" grounds do NOT apply here.

---

## 0. The two Albany camera programs and their ACTUAL governing statutes

This is the single most-misattributed point, so get it right:

- **School-Zone Speed (SZS) cameras — Albany run under VTL § 1111-d**, the
  Albany-specific statute enacted by **Chapter 500 of the Laws of 2023**
  (S.6802 / A.7043, Sen. Breslin). It expressly defines `"the city" = the city of
  Albany`, caps the program at **20 school zones**, and **repeals Dec 31, 2028**.
  ([NY Senate bill S6802](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2023/S6802/amendment/A),
  [LegiScan bill text](https://legiscan.com/NY/text/S06802/id/2804994),
  [ypdcrime VTL Art. 24 index — "1111-D (Albany)"](https://ypdcrime.com/vt/article24.php))
  - VTL § 1180-b is the **NYC** speed-camera statute — do NOT cite it for an
    Albany SZS ticket. Its requirements are closely mirrored in § 1111-d, but the
    correct Albany citation is § 1111-d / Ch. 500 of 2023.
- **Red-Light (RLC) cameras** are authorized under the § 1111-a /
  General Municipal-Law § 370 framework as extended to Albany. The structural
  rules (notice contents, technician certificate, stolen-vehicle/lessor defenses,
  malfunction defense) track **VTL § 1111-a**.
  ([VTL § 1111-a](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/laws/VAT/1111-A))

Both are adjudicated by the **Albany Parking Violations Bureau (PVB)**, which is
authorized to dispose of parking, RLC, and SZS violations only.
([albanyny.gov PVB](https://www.albanyny.gov/403/Parking-Violations-Bureau))

## 1. What a camera ticket is (and is NOT)

- It is **civil owner-liability**, not a moving violation. Per Albany: the
  **registered owner** is liable; the penalty is **$50** (+$25 late);
  **no DMV points, not reported to the State or your insurer.**
  ([albanyny.gov Safer Streets / SZS FAQ](https://albanyny.gov/2355/Safer-Streets))
- Because it's not a conviction, there is **no "I wasn't driving" identity
  defense by itself** — liability attaches to the owner regardless of who drove
  (§ 1111-a(b); § 1111-d). The defenses are about the CITY's proof and a short
  list of statutory affirmative defenses — not about who was behind the wheel.
- **Honest expectation-setting (from the City itself):** the PVB "does not reduce
  or excuse RLC or SZS tickets absent a legal defense (such as a stolen vehicle or
  license plate) or a valid explanation (such as an evidence-supported medical
  emergency)," and the video "is almost always clearly shown."
  ([albanyny.gov PVB](https://www.albanyny.gov/403/Parking-Violations-Bureau))
  So the app must be candid: camera tickets are HARDER to beat than parking
  tickets, and the win comes from the City failing a technical/procedural
  requirement or a true affirmative defense — not from "I didn't do it."

## 2. SCHOOL-ZONE SPEED (SZS) — verified defenses, ranked

Authority: VTL § 1111-d / Ch. 500 of 2023, plus Albany's published program rules.

### Tier 1 — element-of-liability failures (City must prove ALL of these)
For SZS liability the City must show every element; if any fails, no liability:

1. **Speed margin: more than 10 mph OVER the posted limit.** Liability attaches
   ONLY if the vehicle was traveling **>10 mph above** the school-zone limit
   (20 mph zones → must be **>30 mph**). At or under 30 in a 20 zone = not
   liable. Check the recorded speed on the notice against this margin.
   ([§ 1111-d / S6802 text](https://legiscan.com/NY/text/S06802/id/2804994),
   [Times Union via r/cars: "$50 citation… exceeding the 20 mph limit by more than 10"](https://www.reddit.com/r/cars/comments/1ge6w51/albany_school_cameras_issue_more_than_12000/))
2. **Enforcement window: school days, 7 a.m.–6 p.m.** A violation recorded
   outside the active hours/dates for THAT camera is not enforceable. Albany
   publishes per-camera summer windows that differ (e.g., 07:30–13:30) — the
   relevant window is the one posted for that location/date, not a blanket
   7–6. (Real example: a citation stamped 5:55 p.m. is inside the 6 p.m. window
   and will NOT win on timing — confirm the actual stamp vs. that camera's
   posted window.)
   ([albanyny.gov SZS FAQ](https://albanyny.gov/faq.aspx?TID=56),
   [albanyny.gov Safer Streets summer windows](https://albanyny.gov/2355/Safer-Streets))
3. **Location within a valid school speed zone** (≤1,320 ft of the school) and a
   camera lawfully placed among the **≤20 authorized zones.** A camera outside an
   authorized zone, or after the program's Dec 31 2028 repeal, has no authority.

### Tier 2 — technical / certification defects (demand the City's proof)
§ 1111-d builds in strict device-integrity requirements. The City must be able to
produce these; demand them and contest if missing/deficient:

4. **Daily self-test.** The system may not be used unless it **passed a self-test
   of its functions that day.** Demand the **daily set-up log** for that camera
   and date (the operator must sign it stating the self-test passed).
5. **Annual calibration certificate.** Each system must undergo an **annual
   calibration check by an independent laboratory that issues a signed
   certificate of calibration**, kept on file until all that year's cases resolve.
   Demand the certificate covering the violation date.
6. **Trained operator + signed daily set-up log** stating date, time, location,
   and that the self-test passed. Missing/defective log undercuts the prima-facie
   certificate.
7. **The § 1111-d sworn technician certificate** is only *prima facie* evidence —
   it can be rebutted. You may inspect the photographs/video, and the City must
   make the images available in the proceeding.

### Tier 3 — signage
8. **"Photo enforced" + speed limit + enforcement-time signage** must be posted,
   in conformance with the **MUTCD**, on advance-warning and/or speed-limit signs.
   Albany states every zone "will be clearly marked with signage showing the
   speed limit, enforcement time, and the fact that the school zone is photo
   enforced." Genuinely absent/non-conforming signage at that location is a
   contestable defect — best supported by a dated photo of the actual signage
   (or its absence).
   ([albanyny.gov SZS FAQ](https://albanyny.gov/faq.aspx?TID=56))

### Tier 4 — statutory affirmative defenses (need proof)
9. **Stolen vehicle / stolen plates** — complete defense if reported stolen to
   police BEFORE the violation and not yet recovered. Mail a **certified copy of
   the police report** to the PVB.
10. **Lessor / rental** — a lessor who pre-filed under § 239 and timely provides
    the lessee's name/address (within 37 days of notice) shifts liability to the
    lessee.
11. **Operator without consent** — narrow; rebuts the consent presumption (hard
    to prove).
12. **Device malfunction** — it is an express statutory defense that the camera
    system was malfunctioning at the time.

### Tier 5 — notice / mailing & deadline procedure
13. **30-day warning grace per camera activation.** During the **first 30 days**
    a newly activated camera is live, only **warnings** issue (no fine). A "fine"
    dated inside that camera's documented warning window is improper.
    ([albanyny.gov SZS FAQ](https://albanyny.gov/faq.aspx?TID=56))
14. **Notice contents** (§ 1111-d, mirroring § 1180-b): owner name/address, plate,
    location, date/time, camera/locator ID, **at least two date-and-time-stamped
    rear images including the same stationary object**, and the **sworn
    certificate.** A notice missing required content is contestable.
15. **Your appeal deadline: 30 days from issuance** for RLC/SZS (vs. 20 days for
    parking). Late appeals "will not be accepted." After 6 months → collections.
    ([albanyny.gov PVB](https://www.albanyny.gov/403/Parking-Violations-Bureau))

## 3. RED-LIGHT (RLC) — verified defenses, ranked

Authority: VTL § 1111-a (structure as applied to Albany).

### Tier 1 — element / proof failures
1. **The underlying § 1111(d) red-light violation must actually be shown.** The
   system must produce **two or more photographs/microphotographs or video** of
   the vehicle in violation. Inspect the images; if they don't clearly show the
   vehicle entering against a steady red, contest.
2. **If the operator was separately CONVICTED of the underlying § 1111(d)
   violation, the owner is NOT liable** (no double liability) — § 1111-a(b).

### Tier 2 — statutory affirmative defenses (need proof)
3. **Signal malfunction** — express statutory defense that the **traffic-control
   indications were malfunctioning** at the time — § 1111-a(n).
4. **Stolen vehicle** — reported stolen before the violation, not recovered;
   certified police report to the PVB — § 1111-a(i).
5. **Lessor / rental** — lessor shifts liability with lease docs + lessee
   name/address within 37 days — § 1111-a(j).
6. **Operator without consent** — narrow; rebuts consent presumption —
   § 1111-a(k)(2).

### Tier 3 — certificate & notice
7. The **sworn technician certificate** is only *prima facie* — the images
   "shall be available for inspection in any proceeding." Demand and inspect them.
8. **Notice contents** (§ 1111-a(g)(2)–(3)): owner name/address, plate, location,
   date/time, camera/locator ID, plus how/when to contest and the default-judgment
   warning. Missing required content is contestable.
9. **Penalty cap:** liability **≤ $50** per violation (+ up to $25 for late
   response) — § 1111-a(e). Not a conviction; no points/insurance — § 1111-a(f).

## 4. What does NOT work on camera tickets (be honest with the user)

- **"I wasn't the driver / you can't prove who was driving."** Irrelevant —
  liability is on the registered OWNER by statute. Not a defense by itself.
- **"There were no points anyway, just pay it"** — that's a reason some people
  pay, not a legal defense.
- **Color/year/make typo arguments** (the § 238 parking-defect grounds) — these
  are PARKING-ticket grounds and do not apply to camera tickets.
- **"The light was yellow / I was barely over"** — without the City's own
  evidence failing, the video usually defeats this. Only the >10 mph margin (SZS)
  or a genuine signal/image problem (RLC) helps.

## 5. How to contest in Albany (procedure)

- **Review the evidence FIRST:** go to **viewcitation.com**, enter the citation
  number + PIN from the notice, and watch the photos/video. (RLC/SZS evidence is
  NOT on rmcpay.com — that's parking only.)
- **Appeal RLC/SZS only via viewcitation.com or by phone 1-855-427-0455.** This
  gets you a **pre-trial conference** date at the PVB (24 Eagle St, Rm 203).
- **Deadline: within 30 days of issuance** — late = not accepted.
- At the conference, raise the specific verified ground (e.g., demand the daily
  set-up log + annual calibration certificate for an SZS device; inspect the RLC
  images; assert a statutory affirmative defense with proof).
- **Do not just pay** — paying ends the matter as an admission.
  ([albanyny.gov PVB](https://www.albanyny.gov/403/Parking-Violations-Bureau))

## 6. App behavior for camera tickets (how to wire this in)

When the user flags the ticket as RLC or SZS:
1. **Do NOT run the § 238 parking error-scan** — it does not apply.
2. **Route evidence review to viewcitation.com** (number + PIN), not rmcpay.com.
3. **Run the camera checklist instead**, in the ranked order above:
   - SZS: speed margin >10 over? inside that camera's posted hours/dates?
     within the warning grace window? then demand self-test log + annual
     calibration cert; check signage; check affirmative defenses.
   - RLC: inspect images for a clear steady-red entry; signal malfunction;
     affirmative defenses; notice-content completeness.
4. **Be candid about odds** — surface the City's own statement that RLC/SZS are
   not excused absent a legal defense or evidence-backed explanation.
5. **Cite ONLY this document.** For SZS cite § 1111-d / Ch. 500 of 2023 (NOT
   § 1180-b). Never assert a device defect the user can't substantiate — instead
   DEMAND the City produce the log/certificate (their burden to have it).
6. **Deadline = 30 days**, appeal channel = viewcitation.com / 1-855-427-0455.
